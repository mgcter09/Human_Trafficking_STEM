What is Human Trafficking?

Human trafficking is the illegal trade of people through force or deception for exploitation. It takes various forms such as sex trafficking, forced labor, child trafficking, and organ trafficking. This issue is significant because it violates human rights, generates enormous profits for criminals, preys on vulnerable populations, fuels organized crime, and poses a threat to global security. Immediate attention is crucial to protect victims, dismantle trafficking networks, and promote justice and dignity for all..

IMPACT AND CONSEQUENCES

What Human Traficcking Victims Endure

Victims of human trafficking endure severe physical, emotional, and psychological consequences. They suffer physical harm, sexual abuse, malnutrition, and exposure to hazardous conditions. Trafficked individuals also experience emotional trauma, depression, anxiety, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). The broader impact on society includes connections to other criminal activities like drug trafficking, arms smuggling, money laundering, and organized crime. Addressing human trafficking is essential to protect victims, prevent further exploitation, and combat interconnected criminal networks.

Information Beneficial to Know!

Human trafficking is a global problem that involves the exploitation of individuals through force, fraud, or coercion for various purposes. Here is a brief overview of different types of human trafficking: Sex Trafficking: Sex trafficking involves the recruitment, harboring, transportation, provision, or obtaining of a person for the purpose of a commercial sex act, where force, fraud, or coercion is used. It predominantly affects women and children. Some key facts include: Approximately 99% of victims trafficked for sexual exploitation are women and girls. (UNODC) It is estimated that there are 4.8 million victims of forced sexual exploitation worldwide. (ILO) Case Study: In 2018, Operation Independence in the United States resulted in the rescue of 84 minors and the arrest of 120 traffickers involved in sex trafficking.
